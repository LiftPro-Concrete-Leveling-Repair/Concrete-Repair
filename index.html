Restoring Concrete Instead of Replacing It

Many homeowners and business owners assume that once concrete sinks are damaged, replacement is inevitable. The problem is that replacement is often the most disruptive and costly path, even when the slab itself can still be saved. LiftPro offers a restoration approach that lifts and levels existing concrete, helping clients avoid demolition, hauling, new pours, and extended cure times. For driveways, walkways, porches, patios, garages, and many commercial surfaces, lifting and leveling can be a smarter solution that preserves what’s already there while correcting the hazards and visual impact caused by settlement. This approach matters because it respects the reality of property ownership. People need access to their garage. Businesses need customers to enter safely. Families need sidewalks and steps that don’t feel dangerous. When replacement forces are unavailable for long periods, repairs get postponed, and the problem often worsens. LiftPro’s method is designed to be practical, helping clients restore functionality without placing their home or business on hold. It is a focused service built around lifting and repair, not pouring new slabs, keeping the work aligned with the company’s specialty and the customer’s immediate needs.



Polyjacking That Fits Your Schedule

LiftPro uses polyurethane foam injection, often called polyjacking, to lift and stabilize sunken slabs. The process begins with small access holes drilled into the affected concrete. Foam is then injected beneath the slab, where it expands to fill voids and raise the concrete in a controlled way. Because the material cures quickly, many surfaces can be used again within hours. That speed is a major advantage for high-traffic areas like driveways and commercial entry zones, where waiting days for cure time can be a real inconvenience. Beyond speed, polyjacking offers a clean and efficient repair footprint. The access holes are small, the lifting is precise, and the results are visible immediately as the slab rises into place. Once the lift is complete, the holes are sealed to leave a smooth finish. For property owners, the experience feels less like a major construction project and more like a targeted correction that restores the surface quickly. It is especially helpful for people who want results without sacrificing their routine, and for businesses that need a repair option that doesn’t interrupt operations for extended periods.
